You will need to use the fwrite function in r to write the concatenated results of the WTCCC1 study (remember: wtccc1_assoc_compl).

11.2 Some closing thoughts

FUMA is a great tool, but it comes with a caveat. It includes a couple of references of which it is not readily clear which variants are included - the authors do provide the codes used on Git, but still, you donâ€™t know which variants precisely are filtered. That is key: perhaps the top variant you discovered is filtered in the reference. This means FUMA will not use it to map SNPs to genes, rather next best variant. This should be in high-LD - but, again, assumptionsâ€¦ And of course, the references used might not match your data well enough. So, my advice: use FUMA (why not be lazy rather than work hard?), but aware of such caveats as I described. All in all, I do think FUMA is very complete, intuitive, and it makes your work publication-ready because it creates just the right file-formats for you too (.png, .svg, .pdf, .jpeg).
